The Story So Far

Promoted by Arvind, Usha & Yash Gupta, L.T. Elevator leverages 17 years of engineering DNA. The company operates core B2B elevators, a growing B2G automated parking subsidiary (LT ParkSmart) — which completed Phase 1 of the largest multi-level car parking project in the North East (₹43 Cr total), inaugurated by the Chief Minister of Meghalaya, with Phase 2 now underway — and is integrating Ricardo Elevators, a D2C home elevator brand. FY26 marks the first year revenue crossed ₹100 Cr, with 21 offices across India and overseas expansion into Malaysia & Australia underway. Ricardo merger in process; financials consolidate from FY27.

06 • Ricardo — The D2C Opportunity

Manufacturing Meets Direct-to-Consumer

Integration on track. Full consolidation expected in FY27.

Why Ricardo

A digital-native brand acquiring 60+ orders monthly with just ₹10-12L/month in ad spend — a channel no large elevator company has cracked.

  • ₹6 Cr/month bookings from 2,500+ monthly leads.
  • Customers pay 95%+ before dispatch — near-zero receivables.
  • 18 branded centres across India, scaling to 30.
  • Production now transitioning to LT's factory — tighter margins, better quality.
The Strategic Logic
  • Cash flow fix: Ricardo operates a positive working capital cycle — advance payments from B2C customers structurally offset the receivables drag in B2G/B2B.
  • National reach: Pan-India retail & distribution network across India through combined LT + Ricardo centres.
  • Margin uplift: Target net margin ~20% vs ~15% in B2B/B2G once at scale.
  • Low dilution: ₹3.5 Cr + 4L shares, just 2-3% of equity.

Ricardo's ₹6 Cr/month order run-rate (annualised ₹70+ Cr) with a 3–6 month delivery cycle is expected to contribute meaningfully to FY27 revenue once consolidation completes.

11 • Key Risks

What Could Break the Thesis

1. Lumpy Business & Quarterly Optics

Revenue is H2-heavy and project-driven — large contracts book unevenly across quarters. PAT margin narrowed marginally (15.8% FY25 → 15.2% FY26) due to timing of margin recognition, not a structural shift. Company has flagged this directly. Risk: investors misread quarters and punish unfairly; or the margin compression turns out to be real.

2. Negative OCF & Stretched Receivables

Consolidated OCF was -₹16.6 Cr in FY26. Trade receivables grew to ₹45.7 Cr as topline scaled. Ricardo's advance-payment B2C model is the structural fix — but it needs to consolidate first. Until then, working capital remains a quarterly watch item.

3. Ricardo Merger — Timeline & Integration

Merger formally underway but not yet complete. Legal or process delays could push revenue consolidation to FY28. Separately, migrating Ricardo's production in-house carries quality and margin risk in the transition period.

4. New Factory — Capacity on Clock

Q4 FY27 go-live target for the integrated facility (2.5x capacity). Any construction delay coinciding with the Ricardo integration makes FY27 a twin execution risk year. No disclosed schedule buffer.
