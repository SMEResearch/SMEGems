Executive Summary

Promoted by Arvind, Usha & Yash Gupta, L.T. Elevator leverages 17 years of engineering DNA. The company operates core B2B elevators, a booming B2G automated parking subsidiary (Park Smart) — which recently executed the ₹63 Cr largest multi-level car parking in the North East, inaugurated by the Chief Minister of Meghalaya — and is integrating Ricardo Elevators, a hyper-growth D2C home elevator brand. FY26 marks a breakout year with overseas expansion into Malaysia & Australia.

02 • Company Journey

17 Years of Engineering DNA

2008 — Founded in Kolkata

Incorporated in Kolkata. Began elevator design, manufacturing, and installation from the ground up with a focus on engineering excellence.

2018–2020 — Scale & Certification

ISO 9001:2008 certified. Expanded to 20+ branch offices. Crossed 8,000+ AMC installations. Park Smart Solutions launched as a wholly-owned subsidiary for automated parking.

Sep 2025 — IPO at ₹78 (183x Oversubscribed)

Listed on BSE SME platform raising ₹39 Cr for capacity expansion and working capital. Listed at a 74.5% premium. Massive retail and institutional demand.

Nov 2025 — Blockbuster H1 FY26

Revenue +140% YoY to ₹47 Cr. PAT +597% YoY to ₹6.45 Cr. EBITDA margins expanded to 21.3%.

FY26 — Breakout Year

Ricardo merger term sheet signed. ₹63 Cr North East parking project executed & inaugurated by CM of Meghalaya. Expanded to Malaysia & Australia. Full-year topline crossed ₹110 Cr+.

05 • Ricardo Deal

Manufacturing Meets D2C

Integration on track. Full consolidation expected in FY27.

The D2C Engine

100% digital acquisition via Meta/Insta. ₹10-12L/month spend generates 2,500+ inquiries and ~60 orders.

  • ₹6 Cr/month inflows — contributing meaningfully.
  • Already receiving inquiries & executing projects.
  • 3–6 month delivery cycle.
Why It Works
  • Cash Flow: B2C pays 95-100% upfront (vs slow B2G).
  • Geography: East (LT) + 18 pan-India centers (Ricardo).
  • Terms: 2-3% dilution; 3-4 year lock-in.
  • Synergies: In-house manufacturing fixes quality & margins.

09 • Key Risks

What Could Break the Thesis

1. Negative OCF

OCF was -₹3 Cr in FY24/FY25. Must improve as B2C advance-payment mix scales up.

2. Working Capital

Inventory days 478. Cash cycle 515 days. Quarterly WC monitoring required.

3. Integration Risk

Migrating Ricardo's outsourced production in-house involves quality and process risks.

4. Dilution Risk

Aggressive M&A relies on planned preferential raise. Pricing and promoter participation are key.
