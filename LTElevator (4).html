The Story So Far

Promoted by Arvind, Usha & Yash Gupta, L.T. Elevator leverages 17 years of engineering DNA. The company operates core B2B elevators, a booming B2G automated parking subsidiary (Park Smart) — which recently executed the ₹63 Cr largest multi-level car parking in the North East, inaugurated by the Chief Minister of Meghalaya — and is integrating Ricardo Elevators, a hyper-growth D2C home elevator brand. FY26 marks a breakout year with overseas expansion into Malaysia & Australia.

06 • Ricardo — The D2C Opportunity

Manufacturing Meets Direct-to-Consumer

Integration on track. Full consolidation expected in FY27.

Why Ricardo

A digital-native brand acquiring 60+ orders monthly with just ₹10-12L/month in ad spend — a channel no large elevator company has cracked.

  • ₹6 Cr/month bookings from 2,500+ monthly leads.
  • Customers pay 95%+ before dispatch — near-zero receivables.
  • 18 branded centres across India, scaling to 30.
  • Production now transitioning to LT's factory — tighter margins, better quality.
The Strategic Logic
  • Cash flow fix: B2C advance payments offset B2G receivables drag structurally.
  • National reach: 18→30 centres give LT its first consumer-facing distribution.
  • Margin uplift: Target net margin ~20% vs ~15% in B2B/B2G.
  • Low dilution: ₹3.5 Cr + 4L shares, just 2-3% of equity.

Expected FY27 addition: ₹60-70 Cr in revenue — fully incremental, on top of LT's organic trajectory.

11 • Key Risks

What Could Break the Thesis

1. Negative OCF

OCF was -₹3 Cr in FY24/FY25. Must improve as B2C advance-payment mix scales up.

2. Working Capital

Inventory days 478. Cash cycle 515 days. Quarterly WC monitoring required.

3. Integration Risk

Migrating Ricardo's outsourced production in-house involves quality and process risks.

4. Capacity Ramp Timing

The new factory (₹300-400 Cr capacity) targets Q4 FY27 go-live. Any construction delay pushes out the growth runway on both elevators and Ricardo.
